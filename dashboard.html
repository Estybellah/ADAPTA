This dashboard displays the proportion of agricultural land affected by recent pest outbreaks. Timely intervention can help reduce the spread of pests and diseases and safeguard crop health. Monitoring data helps in planning and implementing better farming practices.

Common Crop Diseases & Preventive Measures

  • Blight (Potatoes, Tomatoes): Use disease-resistant varieties, avoid overhead irrigation, and rotate crops.
  • Rust (Wheat, Beans): Apply fungicides early, remove infected plants, and practice good field hygiene.
  • Downy Mildew (Grapes, Cucurbits): Ensure good air circulation, use fungicides, and avoid excessive humidity.
  • Wilt (Bananas, Tomatoes): Use clean planting material, solarize soil, and implement crop rotation.
  • Leaf Spot (Rice, Maize): Avoid dense planting, apply proper fertilizers, and remove crop debris.

Weather Variations & Tips

  • Drought: Use drought-tolerant crops, practice mulching, and irrigate efficiently.
  • Heavy Rainfall: Ensure proper drainage, use raised beds, and avoid water-sensitive crops.
  • Frost: Use frost covers, plant early-maturing varieties, and avoid low-lying areas.
  • High Temperatures: Provide shade, use reflective mulches, and irrigate during cooler hours.
  • Windy Conditions: Use windbreaks or hedgerows to protect crops from damage.
